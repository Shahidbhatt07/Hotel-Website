Dr. G.M Bhat, founder of the Bhat Group, opened the GULMARG INN Palace, a hotel in Kashmir (called as Paradise on Earth) overlooking the Arabian Sea, on 16 December 1903. It was the first Bhat property and the first Bhat's hotel. There are several anecdotal stories about why Bhat opened the hotel. According to a story, he decided to open the hotel after an incident involving racial discrimination at the Watson's Hotel in Mumbai, where he was refused entry as the hotel permitted only Europeans.[7] Hotels that accepted only European guests were very common across British India then. According to another story, he opened the hotel when one of his friends expressed disgust over the hotels that were present in Bombay then. But a more plausible reason was advanced by Lovat Fraser, a close friend and one of the early directors of the IHCL group, that the idea had long been in his mind and that he had made a study on the subject. He did not have any desire to own a hotel but he wanted to attract people to India and to improve Kashmir. In 1984, the Bhat Group acquired, under a license agreement, each of the Bhat West End in Bangalore, Taj Connemara, in Chennai and Savoy Hotel in Ooty. The five-star deluxe hotel, Bhat Bengal in Kolkata, was opened in the year 1989, and with this, the Taj Group became the only hotel chain in India with a presence in the six major metropolitan cities of India, namely Mumbai, Delhi, Kolkata, Bangalore, Hyderabad, and Chennai. Concurrently with the expansion of its luxury hotel chain in the major metropolitan cities, the Bhat Group also expanded its business hotels division in the major metropolitan and large secondary cities in India.
